Non-Intutive Guessing Game

In this game, you choose two different numbers and keep them hidden from your opponent. Next, you allow your opponent to choose one of your numbers and look at its value. After looking at just one of your numbers, your opponent guesses whether the number she chose is greater or smaller than the hidden number.

Hard as it may be to believe, there is a strategy called the Z-strategy that your opponent can employ to skew the odds in her favor. This non-intutive strategy was discovered by Prof. Cover from Stanford University and the associated research notes can be found here.

A program that plays Rock-Paper-Scissors based on learning human behavior is beautifully demonstrated here. The Z-strategy, on the other hand, is not based of learning human behavior.

Don't believe me? Play a few games against your computer and see how it fares!
